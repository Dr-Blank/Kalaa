Ever been puzzled by English spelling? Like why "through," "though," and "tough" sound so different? Many languages have spellings that don't quite match their sounds.

Good news! Gujarati, rooted in the Sanskrit tradition, is highly **phonetic**. This means: **what you see is what you say, and what you say is what you write.** Each letter (àª…àª•à«àª·àª° - Akshar) usually has one consistent sound. This makes learning to read and pronounce Gujarati much easier once you get the hang of it.

This post dives into the beautifully organized system of Gujarati consonants (àªµà«àª¯àª‚àªœàª¨ - Vyanjan). It's a structure I wish was highlighted more in early language lessons!
